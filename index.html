who I am

I'm a Mechatronics Engineering student at TMU, transferred from Computer Engineering because I kept being more drawn to the hardware side of everything. The intersection of electronics, firmware, and physical systems is where I want to live.

On the technical side I've been building with embedded C++ firmware, working through bare-metal peripherals, FreeRTOS task scheduling, sensor fusion with complementary filters, and writing tests that actually catch things before they break in hardware.

Outside of engineering, I've spent years doing real work in clinical data management, youth programs, and community operations. I'm not precious about what kind of problem I'm solving — I just want to solve it well.
